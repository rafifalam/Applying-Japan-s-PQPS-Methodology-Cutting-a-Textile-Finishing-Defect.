# Applying Japan's PQPS Methodology: Cutting a Textile Finishing Defect by 60%

**Category:** Quality Control | Root Cause Analysis | Process Improvement
**Role:** QC Methodology Mentor — trained team in AOTS/Japan PQPS problem-solving

## Summary

Mentored a QC Circle using Japan's AOTS PQPS methodology — root-cause analysis, Pareto ranking, and PDCA-based countermeasures — to fix a textile finishing defect. Cut defect rate from 4.7% to 1.87%, saved ~$1,510 in reprocessing, and won a national Platinum QC award.

## The Problem

At Fakhruddin Textile Mills Ltd (Urmi Group), the finishing division was losing product to **"peach uneven"** — an inconsistent surface texture on mechanically peach-finished fabric, caused by uneven sanding roll-to-roll or meter-to-meter. Over a 5-month baseline, this defect averaged **4.7%** of output, driving reprocessing costs and delivery risk on production for brands including M&S, Puma, and Decathlon.

## The Approach

Rather than leaving the shop floor to troubleshoot ad hoc, structured quality problem-solving methodology (trained through AOTS Japan's **PQPS — Program for Quality Problem Solving**) was introduced, and a 4-person QC Circle team ("Kaizen") was mentored through the full cycle:

| Step | Method | Outcome |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1. Project selection | Brainstormed 5 candidate defects, scored each on cost/feasibility/importance via weighted matrix | "Peach uneven" objectively prioritized as #1 issue |
| 2. Root cause mapping | Fishbone (Man/Method/Material/Machine) + T-diagram breakdown | 9 potential causes identified |
| 3. Pareto analysis | Scored and ranked all 9 causes | 2 causes (peach roller fiber length, fabric tension variation) = 76% of the problem |
| 4. Countermeasure design | 5W1H framework with assigned owners and deadlines | Operator care protocols, 2-month roller replacement cycle, fixed tension at first roll, flat-lock sewing for roll joins |
| 5. PDCA governance | Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle over ~3.5 months | Structured phases for root-cause analysis, solution testing, standardization |
| 6. Standardization | Converted countermeasures into formal SOP | Floor-level training for all peach machine operators and helpers |

## The Result

- **Defect rate:** 4.7% → 1.87% within 3 months
- **Cost savings:** (~$1,510) in reprocessing costs
- **Recognition:** Platinum Award, 24th National Annual Quality Convention (NAQC), Bangladesh Society for Total Quality Management

## Why It Matters

This demonstrates the ability to bring formal, internationally-trained quality methodology (Japanese TQM/PQPS approach) into a real production environment — not just applying tools, but building the structure and mentoring a team to use them independently and sustainably.
